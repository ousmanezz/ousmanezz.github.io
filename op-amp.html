Analog Circuits · ELE 3151

Discrete Op-Amp Design

BJT (2N3904) Current mirror Differential pair Multisim Characterization

Overview

I designed an operational amplifier from discrete components — no op-amp IC — and then characterized it the way you would a real part. Starting from a biased current mirror, I added a differential pair and two common-collector output stages, then measured differential and common-mode gain, CMRR, slew rate, output swing, input bias current, offset, and bandwidth, comparing every result against simulation.

What I built

1) Current-mirror bias network

  • Designed a current mirror for an 800 µA bias at ±12 V rails using 2N3904 BJTs, then added emitter degeneration to stabilize it against load.
  • Swept the load resistance from 10 Ω to 500 kΩ and showed the degenerated mirror held ≈800 µA far more consistently than the basic version.

2) Differential pair & output stages

  • Connected a differential amplifier to the current source, then added two common-collector stages — one to cancel DC offset, one as a buffer to reduce output loading.
  • Built and simulated the full amplifier in Multisim before moving to the bench.

3) Full characterization

  • Measured differential gain, common-mode gain and CMRR, slew rate, output voltage swing, input bias current, output offset, and open-loop bandwidth.
  • Evaluated the amplifier in inverting, non-inverting, and buffer configurations at 2 V/V and 10 V/V, sweeping frequency to find each closed-loop bandwidth.

Results

  • Differential gain matched simulation within ~3%; CMRR measured ≈78 dB.
  • Closed-loop gains tracked the nominal values closely at low gain (under 10% error at 2 V/V), with larger deviation at 10 V/V — traced to the amplifier's deliberately low ~28 V/V open-loop gain.
  • The current mirror landed within ~1% of its 800 µA target and proved insensitive to BJT swap, confirming a robust bias.

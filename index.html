About

What I work on

I am an embedded and firmware engineer at heart. Most of my work lives where hardware meets software. I bring up new boards, write the firmware that drives them, and validate the result on the bench until it behaves. I am comfortable across the stack, from configuring SPI, I2C, and ADC peripherals in bare metal C, to wrapping a graphics library into reusable UI widgets, to standing up BLE connectivity and emulating production hardware for app integration testing.

Two internships at Enerpac have had me build responsive embedded interfaces on Zephyr RTOS, architect BLE GATT services, and contribute to design reviews through DFMEA. Outside of work I gravitate toward edge AI, FPGA design, and analog circuits, projects that push me to understand a system top to bottom. I am based in Milwaukee and graduating in May 2026.

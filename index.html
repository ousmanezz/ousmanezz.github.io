About

What I work on

I'm an embedded and firmware engineer at heart. Most of my work lives where hardware meets software — bringing up new boards, writing the firmware that drives them, and validating the result on the bench until it behaves. I'm comfortable across the stack, from configuring SPI/I2C/ADC peripherals in bare-metal C to wrapping a graphics library into reusable UI widgets, to standing up BLE connectivity and emulating production hardware for app integration testing.

Two internships at Enerpac have had me build responsive embedded UIs on Zephyr RTOS, architect BLE GATT services, and contribute to design reviews through DFMEA. Outside of work I gravitate toward edge AI, FPGA design, and analog circuits — projects that force you to understand a system end to end. I'm based in Milwaukee and graduating in May 2026.
